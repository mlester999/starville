# Phase 12D visual replacement priorities

Status: planning only. Phase 12D is not implemented. The priorities remain provisional until the
pending signed-in renderer screenshots and owner visual-acceptance matrix are complete.

Every bundled entry remains a repository-owned `technical_baseline`. This list prioritizes visual
replacement after Phase 12C scale, composition, camera, shadow, and HUD work; it is not permission
to upload, activate, publish, or delete an asset. Stable keys, immutable pins, anchors, collision,
interactions, source provenance, and the Starville art bible remain the replacement contract.

## Phase 12C handoff contract

Phase 12D inherits the canonical 96×48 projection, shared character-relative scale, bottom-center
foot/depth anchors, upper-left light, lower-right layered soft contact-shadow treatment, target
authored shadow media, bounded camera apron, exact asset pins, and production-renderer Game Test. It
must preserve those contracts unless an explicit versioned policy change is reviewed across the
asset pipeline, runtime, Composer, Draft Preview, readiness, and Game Test.

The unpublished Lantern Square draft now provides 47 objects, social seating, landmark hierarchy,
perimeter clusters, and 36 collision shapes. Shared readiness returns no errors or warnings and the
expected temporary-art recommendation. That proves structural composition readiness only; it does
not approve the current media as final art or replace actual browser comparison.

## A. Acceptable baseline through early beta

These are clear, low-risk technical materials that can remain while higher-identity artwork is
produced:

- `system.missing-asset` as the protected diagnostic fallback (never as normal scenery);
- small UI category/status symbols such as `ui.currency.dust`, inventory/category icons, direction,
  spawn, exit, warning, validation, and social-operation icons;
- low-prominence route signs and boundary markers: `brooklight-sign`, `orchard-road-sign`,
  `closed-route-marker`, and `whisperpine-gate`;
- alias-only ready crop keys, provided they continue to resolve to the same approved authored stage.

They still require contrast, accessibility, and small-size review. “Acceptable baseline” does not
mean final art.

## B. Needs refinement before replacement

These are compositionally useful and now receive Phase 12C renderer scale, grounding, palette, and
repetition treatment:

- terrain and route materials: grass variants, dirt, stone path, plaza, soil states, bridge, and
  water;
- cottages, trees, rock, moonstone, flowers, bush, fence, lamps, and notice board;
- farming plot and crop-growth stages whose silhouettes already communicate authoritative state;
- current furniture whose footprint, rotation, and interaction contracts are useful;
- non-identity workstation active/ready variants and General Store highlight states.

The current refinement includes shared scale normalization, contact shadows, path/water edge
treatment, deterministic variation, clearer base grounding, and palette cohesion. Do not replace
these indiscriminately before comparing the refined baseline in the protected real renderer. Final
doors, roofs, windows, silhouettes, shoreline variants, and animated states remain Phase 12D work.

## C. Needs Phase 12D replacement

These carry the strongest identity or visibly expose development-marker origins:

1. Final modular player/NPC character artwork and authored eight-direction idle, walk, and jog
   animation. The Phase 12C procedural character remains a readable, uniformly scaled foundation
   only.
2. `phase7-general-store-marker`, `phase7-cooking-hearth-marker`,
   `phase7-crafting-workbench-marker`, and `phase7-home-entrance-marker`, with their current state
   variants reconciled into production landmark families.
3. `phase10b-wardrobe-mirror-marker` and `phase10b-wardrobe-furniture-marker`.
4. Player-facing inventory/tool/ingredient/recipe entries whose stable keys still contain `dev` or
   whose silhouette is a generic technical symbol.
5. Primary Lantern Square buildings and social landmarks after the owner accepts final shape
   language, roof/door perspective, signage, and night-light treatment.
6. Water shoreline/corner animation and authored path/plaza adjacency variants after the renderer
   contracts are proven.

The replacement order is character readability, primary landmarks, interaction workstations,
player-held/economy items, then environmental variety. This prevents polished tertiary decoration
from masking weak player and navigation identity.

## D. Blocking

No checked-in bundled file is currently classified as an unconditionally blocking source asset. A
specific version becomes blocking if validation demonstrates any of the following:

- its anchor or visual base cannot agree with the logical footprint;
- it hides a required route or makes collision materially ambiguous;
- it exposes dark void, breaks camera framing, or destroys character readability;
- it has unsafe/copied provenance, missing immutable media, unsupported fake rotation, or a broken
  fallback;
- it exceeds the approved render/download/effect budget; or
- it makes authoritative state indistinguishable.

A blocking finding stops that visual version from approval or activation. It does not authorize an
automatic map rewrite, collision change, hosted asset activation, world publication, or deletion of
history.
